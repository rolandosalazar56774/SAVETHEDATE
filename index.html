Getting There

Travel Information

We want your journey to feel effortless. Below is guidance to help you plan.
Hotel and airline names are examples — always compare rates.

Nearest Airport

Fly into Honolulu International (HNL) — the primary gateway to Oahu, just minutes from Waikiki. Most major U.S. cities offer direct or one-stop service.

Primary Airport

From Texas

Direct flights from Dallas/Fort Worth (DFW) and Houston (IAH) to Honolulu run 8–10 hours. Hawaiian Airlines, American, and United offer regular service. Book early — Hawaii routes fill quickly.

Example Guidance

Book Early

Hawaii flights during popular travel windows can sell out or spike in price. We recommend booking 6–9 months ahead. Specific travel dates will be shared with the official date announcement.

Tip

Luxury Tier

The Royal Hawaiian, Moana Surfrider, and Halekulani offer iconic Waikiki experiences with world-class service. Expect $400–$900+ per night. These iconic properties book well in advance.

Example Options

Mid-Range

Waikiki Beach Marriott, Hilton Hawaiian Village, and Hyatt Regency Waikiki offer great amenities for $200–$400/night — all within easy walking distance of main attractions.

Example Options

Group Block

We are exploring a hotel room block for guests at preferred rates. Details will be announced with the official date. Check back here for booking links when available.

Airport to Waikiki

HNL is 20–30 minutes from Waikiki. Rideshare (Uber/Lyft), taxis, and TheBus public transit all serve the route. Rental cars aren't necessary for Waikiki-based guests.

Info

Getting Around

Waikiki is highly walkable — most restaurants, beaches, and attractions are within 10 minutes on foot. For Diamond Head, Hanauma Bay, and North Shore, rideshare is easy and affordable.

Tip

Wedding Transport

Transportation details for wedding events will be shared with the full schedule. We'll ensure every guest can travel between venues comfortably. More information coming soon.

Coming Soon

Hawaii Time

Hawaii Standard Time (HST) is UTC−10 and does not observe Daylight Saving. Plan accordingly — arriving a day or two early helps with adjustment. Most guests from Texas will be 5–6 hours behind.

Tip

What to Pack

Hawaii runs mid-70s to mid-80s°F year-round. Pack light breathable clothing, swimwear, reef-safe sunscreen, and comfortable walking shoes. A light jacket is useful for evenings or air-conditioned venues.

Packing Tip

Make It a Trip

Hawaii deserves more than a weekend. Consider arriving early or staying after. Diamond Head, Hanauma Bay snorkeling, North Shore surf, and the Polynesian Cultural Center are unforgettable.

The Destination

Explore Waikiki

Your guide to paradise

Beach

Waikiki Beach

The iconic stretch of golden sand with Diamond Head as its backdrop. Perfect for swimming, surf lessons, and golden hour walks.

Scenic

Diamond Head

Hike the iconic volcanic crater for panoramic views of Honolulu. Arrive early for cooler temps and fewer crowds.

Activity

Hanauma Bay

A protected marine sanctuary 20 minutes from Waikiki. Snorkel among hundreds of tropical fish above ancient coral.

Dining

Ocean-View Dining

From fresh poke bowls and Hawaiian plate lunches to Michelin-recognized fine dining overlooking the sunset.

Activity

Surf Lessons

Waikiki's gentle rolling waves have been the perfect learning ground for over a century. Certified instructors available for all levels.

Family

Polynesian Cultural Center

An immersive journey through the cultures of Polynesia — excellent for families and first-time Hawaii visitors.
